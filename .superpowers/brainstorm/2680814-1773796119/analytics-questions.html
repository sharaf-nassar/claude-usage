What should analytics answer?

Select all the questions that feel most useful for a Claude Code user to have answered at a glance. These are grouped by your two priorities.

A. "Am I being productive?" — Story & Health

1

How efficient am I?

Tokens spent per line of code produced. Shows whether Claude is spinning wheels or making progress. Trending up = less efficient, trending down = you're getting more done per token.

2

Am I using my context well?

Cache hit ratio over time — high cache hits mean Claude is reusing context rather than re-reading everything. A dropping ratio might mean sessions are getting fragmented.

3

What's my coding velocity?

Lines of code changed per hour/day with trend. Not just totals — the rate of change, so you can see productive bursts vs idle periods.

4

Am I pacing my usage well?

Time above 80% utilization, projected time to limit, and a simple "health" indicator. Answers "should I slow down?" without needing to interpret a chart.

5

Are my sessions getting longer/bigger?

Average session duration and token size trending over time. Long, token-heavy sessions might mean you're not compacting or starting fresh often enough.

B. "What am I actually doing?" — Workflow Insight

6

What tools am I using most?

Top tools distribution (Edit, Read, Bash, Grep, etc.) — reveals your workflow pattern. Are you mostly reading code? Mostly editing? Heavy on bash commands?

7

Where am I spending my time?

Project-level token and session distribution — which projects consume the most resources? Helps prioritize where Claude is most/least useful.

8

When am I most active?

Activity heatmap or hourly distribution — shows your productive hours and usage patterns. Are you a morning coder? Do you burn tokens late at night?

9

How much is Claude talking vs listening?

Output-to-input token ratio — high output ratio means Claude is generating a lot per prompt. Shows whether you're in "ask short, get long" or "paste context, get edits" mode.

10

Is Claude learning my patterns?

Learned rules count, confidence growth, new rules emerging — shows whether the learning system is actually capturing useful patterns from your workflow.
